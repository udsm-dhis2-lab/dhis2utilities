D.2 Preparation & Administration of Injectable Artesunate for Severe Malaria (Demonstration/Simulation)

Instructions:  Observe health care provider who regularly treats severe malaria patients while they prepare and administer injectable artesunate through observation of an actual patient interaction. Do not interrupt unless the patient is critically ill and needs immediate intervention/attention or if the practice of the clinician will put the patient in danger. If no patient is present, request health care provider to demonstrate as a simulation.

Step 1: Gets the contents correctly out of the package (check for the availability of the following three items: sodium bicarbonate ampoule, Artesunate vials and 5% Dextrose/0.9 saline ampoule)
Step 2: Draws 1ml of sodium bicarbonate into the syringe
Step 3: Adds 1ml of sodium bicarbonate into Artesunate vials (60mg)
Step 4: Shakes for 2-3 minutes
Step 5: Waits until completely dissolve and solution is clear
Step 6: Draws into syringe 5% Dextrose/0.9 saline (2ml for IM; 5ml for IV)
Step 7: Adds the contents into Artesunate vial
Step 8: Shakes for 30 seconds
Step 9: Determines the dose (round up to the nearest ml)
Step 10: Draws the required dose into the syringe
Step 11: Discarded al the syringes and needles into safety box immediately after use
Step 12: Records the procedure in the injection register
Absence of any diluted Artesunate not used within one hour from reconstitution in the injection area
SUB-SCORE: D.2 Inj. Artesunate: Demonstration/Observation

D.3 Administration of Injectable Artesunate for Severe Malaria (Knowledge)

Instructions: ONLY complete the knowledge assessment if you were unable to conduct an observation. Give the knowledge test to a staf person who regularly treats severe malaria patients. For each question below, enter ‘Yes’ or ‘No for whether HCW correctly answers the question. The correct answer is provided for each question for your reference and should not be read out to the HCW until after the test is fnished.

PREPARATION AND ADMINISTRATION OF INJECTABLE ARTESUNATE (KNOWLEDGE)
Were you able to complete a demonstration or observation?
If you were able to do a demonstration or observation you do NOT need to complete this knowledge test.
Artesunate powder should be reconstituted with 1 mL of normal saline (NS)? Answer: No, reconstitution should be with 1 mL of 5% NaHCO3.
Once the artesunate has been reconstituted, then the solution is diluted to the correct treatment concentration using NS or 5% dextrose (D5W)? Answer” Yes. Avoid dilution with sterile water.
The fnal concentration for intravenous (IV) injection is artesunate 10 mg/ml? Answer: Yes, this is correct.
The fnal concentration for intramuscular (IM) injection is artesunate 25 mg/ml? Answer: No, the correct dose for IM use is 20 mg/ml.
Children weighing less than 20 kg should receive a lower parenteral dose of artesunate than larger children and adults? Yes [1] No [0] Answer: No. Smaller children < 20 kg require a higher dosage to maintain an equivalent therapeutic efect
The correct artesunate dose in children <20 kg in weight is 3 mg/ kg/dose? Answer: Yes.
The correct artesunate dose in children >20 kg and adults is 2.4 mg/kg/dose? Answer: Yes.
IV artesunate can be injected directly into the IV port without flushing? Answer: No. The line must be flushed with at least 3 mL of NS or D5W.
IM artesunate must be injected into the gluteus? Answer: No. The anterior thigh (vastus lateralis) is the preferred site of injection in younger children.
IM artesunate is always administered to children on a single injection site. Answer: No. If the total volume to be injected is more than 5ml, it must be divided into two injection sites
